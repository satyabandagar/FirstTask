Kabaddi

Kabaddi is a traditional Indian outdoor sport that originated in ancient India. It is a team game played between two teams of seven players each. The main objective is for a raider to enter the opponent’s half, tag as many defenders as possible, and return safely to their own half while continuously chanting “kabaddi.” The game requires strength, speed, breath control, and quick decision-making. Kabaddi is widely played across India and has gained international recognition through the Pro Kabaddi League.

Kho-Kho

Kho-Kho is an indigenous Indian sport that originated in Maharashtra. It is a fast-paced outdoor team game that emphasizes agility, reflexes, and teamwork. Each team consists of twelve players, with nine active players on the field at a time. The chasing team tries to tag members of the running team using quick directional changes and strategic movements. Kho-Kho is commonly played in schools and colleges and is known for developing physical fitness and coordination.

Mallakhamb

Mallakhamb is a traditional Indian sport that combines elements of gymnastics, yoga, and martial arts. It originated in Maharashtra and is performed on a wooden pole or rope. Players execute various poses, balances, and dynamic movements using strength, flexibility, and body control. Mallakhamb helps in improving concentration, endurance, and overall physical fitness. It holds significant cultural importance and represents India’s ancient fitness traditions.
